Below is a summary table showing the performance metrics (Mean Absolute Error and R Score) for all three datasets:

Results Interpretation

  • Height vs. Weight
    The linear regression model performed very well on the height and weight dataset. The R score of 0.86 indicates that the model explains 86% of the variance in weight based on height. The mean absolute error is relatively low, showing that predictions are close to actual values.

  • Brain Weight vs. Head Size
    The R score is 0.71, suggesting a strong linear relationship between head size and brain weight. However, the mean absolute error is higher, which is expected due to the scale of the data (grams). The model is able to capture most of the variance but not as accurately as in the previous dataset.

  • Boston Housing (MEDV prediction)
    For the Boston Housing dataset, the model achieved an R score of 0.70, meaning it explains 70% of the variance in house prices. The mean absolute error is 3.04, which, given the scale of MEDV, is a reasonable performance for a simple linear regression.

General Observations

  • All three models performed well, with R scores above 0.70, indicating a strong fit for simple linear regression.
  • The best performance (highest R, lowest error) was observed in the Height vs. Weight dataset, which is expected due to its clear linear relationship.
  • More complex datasets (like Boston Housing) may benefit from using more advanced regression techniques or additional feature engineering.
